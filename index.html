Depression Awareness

About Depression


Depression is a very common and serious mental illness. It affects more than 264 million people. Depression can cause various emotional and physical problems; depression can result in feelings of sadness, loss of interest in activities that once used to bring joy, inability to properly learn at school and more. Depression needs to be taken completely seriously because it can affect physical health and even cause suicide. It is important to understand the symptoms of depression (anxiety, disturbed sleep & appetite, lack of self-love, etc) so you can help out family members and peers who may be depressed. Raising awareness of depression, the main goal of this website, is also extremely important so that people know how to get help, are more knowledgeable on the subject and can contribute to getting rid of the stigma around depression. Take the depression self-test form to see if you exhibit symptoms of depression. Do NOT self-diagnose based on the results of the test. The test will instead help you determine whether you need to keep track of your symptoms and it will tell you what can you do to relieve your symptoms if you have any. Visit the resources page if you want to learn more about the topic of depression. If you have any questions or comments make sure to post them on the discussions page!

Resources to learn more


https://adaa.org/understanding-anxiety/depression

Gives a wonderful description on depression and anxiety, while also differentiating between the two. Has links to really helpful webinars and blog posts.

https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/depression

Greatly describes what depression is, how do you know if you are depressed, how can you prevent it and what can you do about if you are depressed.

https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/depression/what-is-depression

Provides in-depth information about depression and similar disorders, treatment options, and how to differentiate between depression and other disorders.

https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/mental-illness/in-depth/mental-health/art-20046477

Great resource on the stigma of mental illness. Tells how to cope with it and how you can help overcome it.

https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/national-helpline

This is a link to a national helpline that provides assistance with people/families dealing with mental disorders or struggling with substance use. Great resource if you want to talk to someone.

https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/depression

Tells what depression is and the symptoms. Use this website if you are interested in talking to a psychiatrist as the website will help you find one. Has many different helpful resources like blogposts and patient stories.

https://www.healthline.com/health/depression/help-for-depression

Great if you are interested in learning about treatment options (like medicine and therapy). Has the numbers of helplines if you want someone to talk to about mental illness.

https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/depression/index.shtml

Gives basic overview of depression. Amazing resource if you want a basic idea of what depression is.
